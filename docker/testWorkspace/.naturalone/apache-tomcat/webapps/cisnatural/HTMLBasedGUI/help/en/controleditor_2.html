A Control ...

... consists of the control's definition (Attributes/Positioning) and its corresponding tag handler (Java class). Typically, the handler class is more or less a stupid sequence of calls to general tag handler classes of the Application Designer framework. For macro controls, you do not have to implement a tag handler class. You can define the inner structure of the control directly with XML. In the case of really new controls, you have to provide for a corresponding tag handler in order to generate your own HTML/JavaScript code.
